Using Machine Learning to Examine Street Green Space and Their Associations with Socioeconomic and Environmental Factors in Los Angeles County.

Yi Sun, Xingzhi Wang Jiayin Zhu (co-second author), Yuhang Jia, Liangjian Chen, Xiaohui Xie, Jun Wu.

Environmental Health Perspective (Under Review)

Abstract (+)

Background: Environmental factors, such as inequitable access to green space, may be one of the potential explanations of health disparities associated with socioeconomic status. Compared to commonly-used green space indicators from downward-facing satellite imagery, street view-based green space estimates may capture different types of green space and represent how environments are perceived and experienced by people on the ground, which is important to elucidate the underlying mechanisms linking green space and health. Objectives: This study aimed to test and evaluate machine learning models that can reliably and efficiently classify the type of vegetation (i.e., tree, low-lying vegetation, grass) from high resolution street view images; and to apply this model to investigate the associations between street view green Methods: This study was set in Los Angeles County at census tract-level. Socioeconomic variables were obtained from the CalEnviroScreen 3.0 dataset. Disadvantaged community was defined as the top 25% scoring census tracts from CalEnviroScreen 3.0. Google Street View images in conjunction with deep learning techniques were applied to measure street view green space and identify vegetation types. The normalized difference vegetation index (NDVI) was used as an alternative estimate of green space. Logistic regression and generalized Poisson regression models were used to examine the association between green space and socioeconomic status (SES), adjusting for population density and rural/urban status. Results: The accuracy of the deep learning model was high with 92.5% mean intersection over union. NDVI were moderately correlated with total street view-based green space and tree, and weakly correlated with low-lying vegetation and grass. All green space indicators were negatively correlated with CalEnviroScreen socioeconomic factors. The results showed significant negative associations between three types of green space and neighborhood SES. The odds of higher street green space among the residents who live in the more disadvantaged neighborhoods was approximately seven times less in comparison to the other neighborhoods (odds ratio: 0.14, 95% CI: 0.11, 0.18). The disadvantaged communities contained 17% less total green space than other communities (incidence rate ratio = 0.83, 95% CI 0.81, 0.84). The most pronounced reduction of green space was observed for low-lying vegetation category. Conclusion: Street view image data coupled with deep learning approach can accurately and efficiently measure eye-level street green space and distinguish different vegetation types. In Los Angeles County, lower SES and racial/ethnic minority communities had substantively less street green space. Governments and urban planners may consider not only the size or density of green space, but also the type and visibility of street green space from pedestrian’s perspective.

Comprehensive Evaluation of the College Students’ Back-to-school Safety Degree Under the Background of the COVID-19 Epidemic.

Xiong Dehui, Zhu Jiayin (co-first author), Li Jiami, Cui Lixin.

IEEE Access (Under Review)

Abstract (+)

Since the outbreak of COVID-19, it has been affecting the normal life of people all over the world. Due to the epidemic, universities adopt the online teaching method. However, this type of teaching mode has a certain adverse effect. It is urgent for university students to return to school. This paper puts forward a comprehensive evaluation of the college students' back-to-school safety degree based on analytic hierarchy process (AHP), which aims to get the students' back-to-school safety degree according to the specific situation of different colleges and universities. Taking the actual data of Beijing Institute of Technology, Tsinghua University, Wuhan University, Shanghai University of Finance and Economics and Chongqing University in June as examples, the results show that the comprehensive evaluation values of the five universities are 0.331 (Level 4), 0.308 (Level 4), 0.741 (Level 2), 0.590 (level 3) and 0.664 (Level 2). The evaluation results of this index system are in line with the reality, and can make a reference contribution to the safety evaluation of colleges and universities during the epidemic period.

Research on Evaluation Indicator System of Customer-perceived Service Quality on WeChat Social Network. [paper]

Jiawei Liu, Yinghui Gao, Lixin Cui, Jiayin Zhu, Yixuan Wu.

Journal of Information and Management, 2019(Z2)

Abstract (+)

Social network can be said to be the third wave of Internet development after network and search engines. Countless examples can be found to prove the influence of social networks on our life. With the advancement of social networking technologies，we have moved our life，work，and entertainment into a new world of social networks，where each of us is a digital immigrant. This paper focuses on the research of WeChat，a representative of the popular mobile social network industry in recent years. Firstly，the paper constructs the initial measurement system for SNS quality by crawling and analyzing 6300 pieces of data from 3 main websites of comments on Weichat. Secondly，combined with a questionnaire survey，the WeChat social network customer perceived service quality evaluation index system is established. . Finally，some strategies and suggestions for improving the service quality on WeChat are put forward.
